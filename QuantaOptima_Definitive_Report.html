Temperature Insights
Low T (0.5): Maximizes interference advantage (up to 3.64 bits/step peak on Levy!) but may sacrifice solution quality by converging too aggressively on multimodal landscapes.
Medium T (2.0): Best balance — achieves lowest error on both Rastrigin and Levy while maintaining meaningful interference (0.39–0.40 avg).
High T (5.0): Most exploration, lowest interference. Best for very rough landscapes where premature convergence is the enemy, but interference advantage is minimal.
Recommendation: T=2.0 (default) is the optimal general-purpose setting. Use T=0.5 for smooth/unimodal problems, T=5.0 for extremely rugged landscapes.
